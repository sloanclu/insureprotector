Voice Recording Overview

All CICA Life of America applications completed over the phone must be on a recorded line. A voice signature is mandatory for the application and all other forms submitted via telephone. It represents your signature and is as legally binding as your actual signature.

Upon completion of the application, agents must upload the call recording to their agency.

Reminder: Before completing the e-app, read the following statement exactly as written in its entirety.
"We will telephonically complete this application and all other forms, including a voice signature. A voice signature represents your signature, provides the same legal and binding effect as your physical signature, and will become part of your application."
NOTE: At the beginning of the appointment, the agent must ask, and record the client's agreement to the following steps in this guide.

Important Notes

  • All applications must be completed through the CICA Life of America e-app.
  • All questions in the e-app must be asked exactly as written.
  • Clients must provide a clear "yes" or "no" response to each question before the agent can move on to the next question.
  • Pre-recorded HIPAA authorization is only available on Standard Issue products.
  • The application recording ID is either:
    • Insured's 10-digit phone number and date of application
    • Or a valid voice recording ID from a voice recording device
    • Not three-digit HIPAA code

For All Product Types

"Do you agree to CICA Life Insurance Company of America accepting your signature electronically through voice recording? Do you understand that by stating 'yes', you are signing the application electronically as if you had signed a paper application?"
Yes response is required.

Standard Issue — HIPAA Authorization

Agents must obtain a HIPAA disclosure and authorization. A pre-recorded authorization is completed after the medical questions. Agents can obtain consent via text, email, or voice through a pre-recording statement.

For Voice Authorizations

Before playing the pre-recorded statement, agents must read the following in its entirety:

"To complete the life application, I need your verbal consent to obtain your medical information. To do so, I will make a conference call using a recording system that will provide the HIPAA authorization."

Steps to Complete

  1. Conference in the pre-recorded statement, obtain the three-digit code from the beginning of the call.
  2. Save the code, but do not continue to next steps until after disconnecting the HIPAA statement.
  3. Play the pre-recorded statement.
  4. Applicant must provide clear verbal consent.
  5. Before disconnecting call and moving to next steps, applicant must provide their full name and complete physical address.
  6. Disconnect from the recording, and validate the recording code.
  7. Mark yes to the applicant agreeing to the HIPAA authorization.

Email or Text Authorization

If an agent selects text or email authorization, they must read the following disclaimer to the proposed insured prior to sending a text or email:

"By providing your cell phone number or email address, you are providing us and our partners with your consent to send you a text message or email for the sole purpose of sending you a link to sign an authorization in conjunction with your application for life insurance with CICA Life Insurance Company of America. This consent is valid for this purpose even if your telephone number is currently listed on any Do Not Call list. Your cellular provider's message and data rates will apply to our message."

Guarantee Issue

For Guarantee Issue applications, agents must read the following statements in their entirety. Applicants must affirm with a "yes."

HIPAA Statement

"By applying my voice signature, I authorize all medical-related service providers involved in the care, diagnosis, and treatment (including doctors, hospitals, insurance companies and the Medical Information Bureau) to disclose medical records with CICA Life Insurance Company of America for the purpose of determining eligibility for insurance, administering coverage and payment of a claim."

Authorization Statement

"By my voice signature, I authorize: (i) any licensed physician, medical practitioner, clinic, hospital or other medical or medically related facility, insurance company, MIB, or other person, organization or institution that has any records or knowledge of me, my health, or my child's health (as applicable), to give to CICA Life Insurance Company of America or its reinsurers any such information and to testify as to such information, and (ii) the Company to conduct investigations at any time before or after any policy issuance with any sources and regarding information as the Company deems relevant to issuance of a policy or any claims made under a policy. I further authorize the Company, or its reinsurers, to make a brief report of my personal health information to MIB or reinsurance companies or other persons or organizations performing business or legal services in connection with this application. I understand that such disclosures are permitted by law."
Yes response is required.

Payment Authorization Form

At times, a payment authorization form may be needed. In those instances agents need to read the following statement in its entirety.

Yes response is required.
"By providing your cell phone number or email address, you are giving CICA Life of America and our partners consent to send you a text message or email solely to provide a link to sign an authorization related to your life insurance application with CICA Life Insurance Company of America. This consent remains valid for this purpose even if your telephone number is currently listed on any Do Not Call List. Your cellular provider's message and data rates will apply."

Closing the Call

NOTE: Before concluding the call, it's important for the agent to inform the applicant of the next steps in the process and express gratitude for their business.
"I have submitted the application. Once issued, the policy number will be (application number). Thank you for your time today; I appreciate your business."

Audits

CICA Life Insurance Company of America will complete a random audit of voice recordings. Inaccuracies, false information, or regulatory violations will be reported immediately for corrective action.

Failure to follow the Voice Recording Guidelines may result in disciplinary action, including but not limited to termination for cause.

Items Reviewed During Audit

  • Is there a recording on file?
  • Was the applicant's name verified?
  • Was the applicant's address verified?
  • Was Agent's name provided?
  • Was Agent's number provided?
  • Was verbal confirmation provided by the applicant that they are requesting the policy?
  • Were the questions answered confirmed?
  • HIPAA authorization — was it read in its entirety to the applicant?
  • Was the confirmation statement provided at the end of the call?
